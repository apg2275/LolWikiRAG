League of Legends V11.23[]

Client[]

  • Rotating Game Mode: Ultimate Spellbook returns to live servers from November 18, 2021 (1:00 PM PT) to January 10, 2022 (11:59 PM PT).
  • Essence Emporium returns from November 18th to December 1st, 2021.
End of Season Rewards
  • Summoner's Rift Ranked and Honor End of Season Rewards will be distributed starting this patch and will be completed by December 16 (Riot Regions and Garena only). Clash End of Season Rewards will be distributed starting this patch and will be completed by December 16 (Riot Regions only). Tencent will distribute Ranked and Clash rewards for players in China during patch 11.24.
Ixtal Clash
  • Team formation for the first weekend of the Ixtal-themed Clash starts on November 15, and the tournament will be on November 20 and 21. Team formation for the second weekend will open on November 29, and the tournament will be on December 4 and 5.
  • Ixtal Clash is not a part of the regular Clash season. You'll still be able to earn Ixtal rewards, but your Clash VP track won't be affected.
Loot Odds
  • Drop rates for content from Loot items, now called Loot Odds, will soon be displayed in the Store on the League client! You can now view Loot Odds directly in the client in addition to the existing Hextech Crafting FAQ webpage so you can make better informed decisions at the time of purchase.
  • Loot Odds will roll out slowly all over the globe starting later in patch 11.23. Certain regions have additional regulations around Loot, so it may take additional time for Loot Odds to appear.

Game[]

All Chat
  • Based on initial results since we originally disabled /all chat in a few regions in patch 11.21, we're continuing the disable where it's already in effect but we're not adding additional regions. From here on out, we'll message any per-region changes to impacted regions only, unless we've got global updates to share.
Objective Bounties
  • New Effect: The team that is sufficiently behind can now receive objective bounties, calculated based on their experience and gold deficit as well as how many more Dragons and turrets the opposing team has secured. The bounties activate 15 seconds after the team falls below the threshold and deactivate the same time after they rise above it, which extends if the team is in-combat with an objective that has an active bounty. They are highlighted on the minimap and the tab menu for both teams. When an objective bounty is claimed, the bounty's gold is distributed evenly among all team members. The bounty's value can increase by up to 60% as a team falls further behind. Objective bounties can activate on the following objectives:
    • Baron Nashor: 500, up to 800
    • Dragons and Elder Dragon: 500, up to 800
    • Rift Herald: 500, up to 800
    • Inner turrets: 400, up to 640
    • Nexus turrets: 400, up to 640
    • Outer turrets: 250, up to 400

Champions[]

Caitlyn - Art & Sustainability Update
  • General
    • Complete visual update across all skins.
    • New splash artwork for Resistance Caitlyn, Sheriff Caitlyn, Safari Caitlyn, Arctic Warfare Caitlyn, and Officer Caitlyn.

    • New voice-over.
    • Added ejected bullet when reloading her rifle.
  • Headshot
    • Removed: Gaining an Empowered Headshot no longer allows Caitlyn to attack the trapped target ignoring an ongoing attack cooldown.
    • Undocumented/New: Because they are proper attacks now, Headshot attacks against trapped enemies also generate a stack towards Headshot now.
    • "Passive Ready" SFX adjusted for more of a Hextech feel. Added SFX that plays when Headshot is ready for use.
  • Piltover Peacemaker
    • New meshes and particles made to look more Hextech. Initial on-hit SFX are now more impactful than subsequent hits.
  • Yordle Snap Trap
    • Added indicator on traps and their activation VFXs. Trap SFXs adjusted to match new animation timing.
  • 90 Caliber Net
    • New range indicator. On-hit SFX are now more impactful.
  • Ace in the Hole
    • New laser and Hextech imagery. On-cast SFX are now more impactful and have additional Hextech elements.
Fiddlesticks
  • A Harmless Scarecrow
    • Bug Fix: Placing a Scarecrow Effigy while channeling Crowstorm no longer overrides the ultimate's SFX.

  • General

  • General

      • Previously, the invisible attack missile that manages interactions such as damaging wards naturally brought along the Energized proc on-attack.
      • Now works like Gangplank's Parrrley or Ezreal's Mystic Shot; When the attack applies on-hit effects and Graves has 100 stacks, it'll apply the effect. This happens even if maximum stacks were reached after the attack was fired or by the attack being fired.

  • Unique Passive - Mana Charge: Grants a charge every 8 seconds, up to 4 charges. Affecting an enemy or ally with an attack or ability consumes a charge and grants 3 bonus mana, increased to 6 if it's a champion, up to maximum of 360 bonus mana. Transforms into Fimbulwinter at +360 mana.
  • Limited to 1 Mana Charge item.

Runes[]

Aftershock
  • New Effect: Tooltip now tracks damage mitigated.
Glacial Augment
  • New Effect:
    • Immobilizing an enemy champion will cause 3 glacial rays to emanate from them towards you and other nearby enemy champions, creating icy zones that slow enemies within by 35% (+ 2% per 100 AP) (+ 2% per 100 bonus AD) (+ 5% per 10% heal and shield power) and reducing their damage by 15% against your allies, excluding yourself, for 3 (+ 100% of the immobilizing effect's duration) seconds.
    • Cooldown: 25 seconds.
  • Old Effect:
    • Glacial Augment: Basic attacks on-hit slow enemy champions by ( 45% − 55% / 30% − 40%) (based on level) for 2 seconds, which increases in effectiveness over the duration. This effect cannot occur on the same target more than once every few seconds.
      • Cooldown: 7 / 6 / 5 / 4 (based on level) (per target) seconds.
    • Freeze Ray: Slowing an enemy champion with an item active creates a 600-unit frozen line in their direction for 5 seconds, slowing all enemies within by 60%. The Freeze Ray can fire upon any number of champions concurrently, and has no cooldown.
First Strike - New keystone rune
  • Replaces Prototype: Omnistone.
  • Inspiration Keystone rune.
    • Passive: Dealing damage or applying a crowd control effect to an enemy champion within the first 0.25 seconds of champion combat, grants 5 and First Strike for 3 seconds, causing all of your post-mitigation damage dealt against champions to deal 10% bonus true damage. Afterwards, you are granted ( 100% / 70%) of all bonus damage dealt within the duration as gold.
      • First Strike will be placed on cooldown after being struck by an enemy champion before you strike them.
    • Cooldown: 25 − 15 (based on level) seconds.
Lethal Tempo
  • New Effect:
    • Basic attacks on-attack against enemy champions grant a stack for 6 seconds, stacking up to 6 times. Gain ( 15% / 5%) bonus attack speed for each stack, up to ( 90% / 30%) bonus attack speed at maximum stacks. At maximum stacks, gain ( 50 / 100) bonus attack range and increase the attack speed cap to 10. Stacks decay by 1 every 0.5 seconds upon expiring.
    • Cooldown: None.
  • Old Effect:
    • After 1.5 seconds upon damaging an enemy champion you gain 40% − 110% (based on level) bonus attack speed and your attack speed cap is increased for 3 seconds. Attacking a champion while the effect is active extends the duration to 6 seconds.
    • Cooldown: 6 seconds, starts after the effect ends.
Prototype: Omnistone - Removed
  • Removed from the game.
  • Replaced by First Strike.

Monsters[]

Elemental Dragons
  • Chemtech Drake - New Elemental Drake
    • When the map transforms into the Chemtech Rift, summons foggy zones within each jungle quadrant. Units within these zones are empowered by Chemtech Fog, which grants them camouflage if they haven't declared an attack, casted an ability, or channeled within the last 1.5 seconds, and up to 10% increased damage against enemies with 340 − 1020 (based on target's level) greater current health than them.
      • Units cannot gain the camouflage while channeling.
  • Hextech Drake - New Elemental Drake
    • When the map transforms into the Hextech Rift, summons pairs of hex-gates within both team's bases and each jungle quadrant. Hex-gates can be interacted with by champions to channel for 0.75 seconds. Upon completion, they blink to the other side of the gate but become vanished for 2.25 seconds. Pairs of hex-gates have a 30-second cooldown after being activated.
      • The channel can be interrupted by taking damage, performing another action, as well as becoming grounded. If this occurs, the hex-gate is placed on a 1.5-second cooldown.
      • The channel cannot be started while unable to cast abilities or grounded.
      • The blink location can be chosen a slight distance away from the gate, otherwise the blink location is always at the gate.
        • This location cannot be over terrain.
      • The gates that lead from the hex-gates within each team's base cannot be interacted with.
Rift Scuttler
  • New Effect: The first Rift Scuttlers spawned now have 35% reduced health, 80% reduced experience and reduced size.

Neutral buffs[]

Dragon Slayer
  • Chemtech Blight - New stacking drake buff
    • + 5 / 10 / 15 / 20% (based on stacks gained from slaying the Chemtech Drake) increased damage against enemies with 340 − 1020 (based on level) greater current health than you.
  • Hextech Prowess - New stacking drake buff
    • + 5 / 10 / 15 / 20 (based on stacks gained from slaying the Hextech Drake) ability haste and 5 / 10 / 15 / 20% (based on stacks gained from slaying the Hextech Drake) bonus attack speed.
  • Cloudbringer's Grace
    • New Effect: + 3.5 / 7 / 10.5 / 14% (based on stacks gained from slaying the Cloud Drake) slow resist and out-of-combat bonus movement speed.
    • Old Effect: + 12 / 24 / 36 / 48 (based on stacks gained from slaying the Cloud Drake) ultimate haste.
Dragon Soul
  • Chemtech Dragon Soul - New Dragon Soul
    • Upon taking lethal damage, enter resurrection for 2 seconds, and afterwards heal for 80% base health (+ 50% bonus health) and become a zombie, during which you gain ( 75% / 30%) decaying bonus movement speed, reduce outgoing damage by 33% and incoming damage by 40%, and take true damage equal to a percentage of the amount of health started with that increases every 0.25 seconds. (300 second cooldown).
  • Hextech Dragon Soul - New Dragon Soul
    • Damaging an enemy with an attack or ability causes them to be struck with lightning, dealing 25 − 75 (based on level) bonus true damage and slowing them by ( 45% / 35%) (+ 3% per 100 bonus AD) (+ 1% per 100 AP) (+ 0.5% per 100 bonus health) decaying over 2 seconds, while chaining the effect to up to 3 enemies (8 second cooldown).
      • The slow can stack from multiple sources, and the decay rate is decreased against ranged champions.

Ultimate Spellbook[]

User Interface
  • Bug Fix: Clicking the "Confirm" button upon selecting an Ult-ernate Summoner Spell will now play an SFX.
Map
  • Removed: No longer uses the Ruined Summoner's Rift map.
Dragons
  • Removed: No longer uses the Ruined Dragon. A dragon of only one type will spawn once every 3:30 minutes.
    • Dragon Slayer grants a buff that deals 0.5 / 1 / 1.5 / 2% (based on stacks) of target's maximum health magic damage once every second against turrets whenever you deal damage to them.
    • Slaying 4 ruined dragons grants the Dragon Soul:
      • Upon taking fatal damage, enter resurrection for 1.5 seconds, during which you are invulnerable, untargetable, and unable to act, and afterwards restore all of your health and take continuous internalraw damage (300 second cooldown).
        • Guardian Angel and Chronoshift will take priority over this effect. Zombie state effects will not.
  • New Effect: The first 2 dragons will now be random. The elemental rift will either be the Chemtech Rift or Hextech Rift and the final dragons will either be the Chemtech Drake or Hextech Drake.
  • New Effect: Slaying epic monsters now grants a buff that deals 0.3% maximum health magic damage once every second against turrets whenever you damage them.
Summoner spells
  • Junglers now have Auto-Smite instead of Placeholder Ult-ernate Summoner Spell in champion select. Auto-Smite gets replaced by the Ult-ernate that they select at the beginning of the game, granting a buff that is also called Auto-Smite.
    • Auto Smite: Causes damage dealt against Blue Sentinel, Red Brambleback, Rift Scuttler, or epic monsters while they are below 450 health to deal 450 bonus true damage.
      • Only affects the Blue Sentinel and Red Brambleback within the user's respective side of the jungle.
    • When Ivern takes Auto-Smite, he will not be able to use it to bypass Friend of the Forest's grove maturation timer. However, freeing a qualifying jungle camp will trigger Auto-Smite, granting him the heal and jungle starter item stack that a regular Smite would.
Items
  • Removed: Emberknife and Hailblade.
  • Obsidian Edge - New item
    • Replaces Emberknife and Hailblade.
    • Requires Placeholder and Auto-Smite to buy.
    • Cost: 350
    • Stats: 8% omnivamp against monsters
    • Unique Passive - Sear: Damaging monsters burns them for 60 (+ 30% AP) (+ 5% bonus AD) (+ 2% bonus health) magic damage over 5 seconds. Omnivamp against monsters provided by this item is not reduced by area of effect damage.
    • Unique Passive - Auto Path: Triggering Auto Smite 5 times consumes this item, granting all of the item's effects permanently as well as increasing the threshold and damage to 900.
    • Unique Passive - Huntsman: Killing large monsters grants 60 bonus experience. The first large monster killed grants an additional 150 bonus experience, for a total of 210. If you are at least 2 levels below the average level of the game, large monster kills grant 50 bonus experience per level below the average level of all players in the game. If your gold from minions is greater than 40% your gold from monsters, a lane minion will grant 13 less gold and 50% reduced experience. This penalty is removed at 20 minutes.
    • Recoup: Regenerate up to 8 − 18 (based on level) mana per second when in the jungle or river, based on missing mana.
