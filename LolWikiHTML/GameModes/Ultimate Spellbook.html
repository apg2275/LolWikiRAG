Ultimate Spellbook

Sign in to edit
  • View history
  • Talk (0)

Ultimate Spellbook is a Featured Game Mode in League of Legends. Players choose one additional, random champion's ultimate ability to deploy in chaotic duels and teamfights.[1]

Overview[]

  • Map: Summoner's Rift 5v5
  • Draft type: 10 Blind-Ban draft
  • Mode: Classic

Features[]

  • One summoner spell slot is occupied by Placeholder Ult-ernate Summoner Spell that cannot be swapped out with another summoner spell. Junglers instead have a Placeholder and Attack-Smite.
  • At the start of the match, you will be given 30 seconds to choose from a pool of 4 ultimates.
    • If you do not confirm to take the ultimate you have selected, that ultimate will be taken automatically.
    • If you do not select any ultimate, the first ultimate is taken by default.
  • After some time, the ultimate you picked takes the place of your Placeholder Ult-ernate Summoner Spell for the rest of the game, and you cannot change it.
    • You can still rank up your own champion's ultimate.
  • All Ult-ernate Summoner Spells do not have resource costs and have an initial cooldown of 130 seconds, increased to 210 if you have Attack-Smite.
    • These ultimates can be used even before reaching level 6.
  • All players have 70 summoner spell haste, 160 − 330 (based on level) bonus health, 15% Tenacity and 40 bonus movement speed out-of-combat.
  • You can see everyone's chosen Ult-ernate Summoner Spells on both the scoreboard and next to their health bar.
  • You start at level 3 and with 950.
  • Passive experience and gold are increased and you share minion gold with teammates in lane.
  • Slaying epic monsters grants a buff that deals 0.3% maximum health magic damage once every second against turrets whenever you damage them.
  • Turret Platings fall off at 9:00.
  • Rift Herald spawns at 6:00.
  • Slaying Baron Nashor leaves behind an Eye of the Herald.
  • Unsealed Spellbook is disabled in this mode.

Attack-Smite[]

  • Junglers have "Placeholder and Attack-Smite" instead of Placeholder Ult-ernate Summoner Spell in champion select. Attack-Smite gets replaced by the Ult-ernate Summoner Spell that they select at the beginning of the game, granting a buff that is also called Attack-Smite.
    • Attack-Smite: Causes basic attacks against Blue Sentinel, Red Brambleback, Rift Scuttler, or epic monsters while they are below 450 health to deal 450 bonus true damage.
      • Only affects the Blue Sentinel and Red Brambleback within the user's respective side of the jungle.
    • When Ivern takes Attack-Smite, he will not be able to use it to bypass Friend of the Forest's grove maturation timer. However, freeing a qualifying jungle camp will trigger Attack-Smite, granting him the heal and jungle starter item stack that a regular Smite would.
Exclusive Jungle item
  • Unavailable Items: Emberknife and Hailblade.
  • Exclusive Items: Obsidian Edge.

One for All-timate Spellbook[]

In each match of the game mode, there is a slight chance for every player's pool of ultimates to contain the same ultimate in all 4 selections; all champions on both teams will have the same Ult-ernate Summoner Spell.

Notes[]

  • Ult-ernate Summoner Spells by default have 35 bonus ability haste and scale with 80% of their regular ratios (unless stated otherwise).
  • Chosen ultimates have modified scalings in this mode, based upon the reduced ratios:
    • Ultimates that scale with AP but not AD, now also scale with AD.
      • These ultimates now scale with 2.414% bonus AD per 1% AP.
    • Ultimates that scale with AD but not AP, now also scale with AP.
      • These ultimates now scale with 0.6% AP per 1% total AD and 0.4% AP per 1% bonus AD.
    • Ultimates that scale with health but not AP nor AD, now also scale with AD and AP.
      • These ultimates now scale with 13.158125% bonus AD and 8.198125% AP per 1% bonus health.
    • Ultimates that do not scale with anything will continue to scale with nothing.
  • Ult-ernate Summoner Spells level up as your champion's personal ultimate levels up to rank 2 and 3 at levels 11 and 16 and benefit from summoner spell haste as well as ultimate haste.
  • Ultimates are treated as summoner spells for the purposes of effects that interact with them, such as Nimbus Cloak.
  • Axiom Arc's Flux does not reduce the cooldown of Ult-ernate Summoner Spells.
  • Ult-ernate Summoner Spells are not applicable to drop as Spell Shards for Zoe's Spell Thief.
  • The following ultimates will grant 5 − 35 (based on level) adaptive force,  bonus armor, and  bonus magic resistance after their initial cooldown has elapsed:
    • Bard's Tempered Fate
    • Gwen's Needlework
    • Janna's Monsoon
    • Kai'Sa's Killer Instinct
    • Poppy's Keeper's Verdict
    • Rell's Magnet Storm
    • Ryze's Realm Warp
    • Taliyah's Weaver's Wall
    • Xerath's Rite of the Arcane
    • Zoe's Portal Jump
  • Your champion's ultimate will not be available in the pool of ultimates.
  • Ultimate selection is unique per player on the same team; each player receives a pool of ultimates that are different from their teammates' pools. Only one type of ultimate may be available in the pool per team.
    • These restrictions do not apply in One for All-timate Spellbook.
    • The leftmost ultimate is weighted depending on a champion's class in order to avoid sub-optimal choices.[2]

Trivia[]

  • Ultimate Spellbook released with a unique Ruined Dragon using Mordekaiser's dragon from Children of the Grave that was then supplanted by Hextech Drake & Chemtech Drake in V11.23. It granted a dragon soul similar to Chemtech Drake.
  • Ultimate Spellbook used a standard Blind pick drafting type in its first launch.
